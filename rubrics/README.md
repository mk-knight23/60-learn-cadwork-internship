## üéØ How to Use These Rubrics

### Before Submission

1. **Self-Assessment**: Rate your work using the relevant rubric
2. **Address Gaps**: Improve areas that score below target
3. **Peer Review**: Have a peer assess your work
4. **Iterate**: Make improvements based on feedback
5. **Final Check**: Verify all criteria meet "Professional" level

### During Review

1. **Use Scoring Guide**: Score each criterion objectively
2. **Provide Evidence**: Reference specific examples for scores
3. **Actionable Feedback**: Give specific improvement suggestions
4. **Set Timeline**: Establish deadline for revisions

---

## üìà Scoring Levels

Each criterion is scored on a 4-point scale:

### üü¢ 4 - Professional (Exceeds Expectations)
- Meets all criteria at a high level
- Demonstrates deep understanding
- Shows attention to detail
- Exemplary work that can be used as reference

### üîµ 3 - Competent (Meets Expectations)
- Meets all criteria adequately
- Shows solid understanding
- Minor improvements possible
- Acceptable for professional work

### üü° 2 - Developing (Needs Improvement)
- Meets some criteria but has gaps
- Shows basic understanding
- Needs significant improvement
- Requires revision before acceptance

### üî¥ 1 - Incomplete (Unacceptable)
- Fails to meet most criteria
- Shows lack of understanding
- Major issues present
- Must be redone

---

## ‚úÖ Minimum Standards

All deliverables must meet the **Competent (3)** level across all criteria to be accepted. Work scoring at **Developing (2)** must be revised and resubmitted.

---

## üìù Customizing Rubrics

Rubrics can be customized for specific projects by:

1. **Adding Criteria**: Include project-specific requirements
2. **Adjusting Weights**: Emphasize certain criteria more heavily
3. **Modifying Descriptors**: Tailor to specific context
4. **Setting Targets**: Establish project-specific score thresholds

All customizations should be documented and agreed upon with your mentor before use.

---

## üîç Review Process

### Step 1: Self-Review
- Complete rubric yourself
- Identify areas for improvement
- Make revisions

### Step 2: Peer Review
- Peer completes rubric
- Compare scores and discuss differences
- Agree on final score

### Step 3: Mentor Review
- Mentor assesses work
- Provides detailed feedback
- Sets revision timeline if needed

### Step 4: Final Revision
- Address all feedback
- Resubmit for final approval
